Interior Design Types

Mid-Century Modern




The mid-1900s produced some of the most iconic pieces in modern design. It is characterized by refined lines, minimalist silhouettes, and natural shapes. From Saarinen, Niemeyer, Eames, Noguchi, Jacobsen and beyond.

Traditional


Traditional design celebrates the long, rich history of the past by juxtaposing it with more modern elements for a decidedly elegant spin on sophistication while highlighting 18th and 19th-century European decor. It’s a timeless go-to style that exudes easy elegance and comfort.

Urban Modern


An urban modern design style is all about cosmopolitan living, or at the very least, cosmopolitan influence. It’s hip, trendy, and ultimately has a very industrial aesthetic, but with a much softer and less edgier side.What an urban modern design strives for, is to give those that live in cities—or anyone with long workdays for that matter—a haven to refresh, be safe, and feel protected and nurtured in.

Industrial


Industrial style can also be seen in the use of unexpected materials used in building. Shipping containers are now being used in architecture for homes and commercial spaces. These are prime locations because they provide almost a blank space for homeowners to get started with a fresh canvas. These locations also contain some of the key elements used to achieve this style of design including exposed bricks and pipes, concrete flooring, and large open windows.

Bohemian


Botanicals fit perfectly with bohemian style as plants are an affordable and easy to source method in creating a relaxed vibe while adding dimension to a room. Plants also do double duty of purifying air while adding a pop of color without interfering too much with the rest of your design and confusing the eye.

Contemporary


In home decorating, if you enjoy things that are modern and like to keep current with the styles of the moment, you are a follower of contemporary style. While it's sometimes thought that "modern" interiors are cold and minimalist, in today's homes, contemporary interiors are comfortable and welcoming without being cluttered and dark.

FarmHouse


Classic, elegant and comfortable, the farmhouse style of decorating is all about keeping things simple and organic. Unlike many other modern interior design styles, this particular approach allows you to decorate with a wide variety of accessories and furnishings that add a touch of inimitable personality to your home.

Modern


Modern design encompasses a lot of different incarnations, which can make it difficult to define. In its simplest terms, modern interior design refers to the reflection of the modern art movement on the interiors of the home. There are several central characteristics and design themes seen throughout modern design, however, that could be described in part as the definition.

Shabby Chic


“Shabby” is the one word that you should never let fool you when it comes to adopting the various aspects of shabby chic interior design to any space. This is one design style that is sure to add a massive level of elegance to your home, though, visually speaking, there doesn’t seem to be very much formality involved.

Interior designers make interior spaces functional, safe,
and beautiful by determining space requirements and select
decorative items, such as colors, lighting, and materials.
They read blueprints and must be aware of building codes
and inspection regulations,as well as universal accessibility
standards.

Interior design is the art and science of enhancing the interior
of a building to achieve a healthier and more aesthetically pleasing
environment for the people using the space. An interior designer is
someone who plans, researches, coordinates, and manages such
projects.
